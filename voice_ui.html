Voice UI Sales App

Objective

To design a voice user interface app for Android, as part of a take-home design task. The app needed to allow users to speak questions into the microphone and be able to respond back.

Approach

- When designing the app, I limited myself to the use case of it being used in a shoe store by a regional manager who travels the country.
- Read through the literature for guidelines on Voice Interface Design, and looked at examples of successful bots, such as the Quartz news app and the Poncho weather app.
- Organised an informal interview with a friend who worked as the manager of a shoe store in order to better understand the kind of reporting she did on a monthly basis.

Design Decisions

- Since this app had functionality that was self-explanatory, the on-boarding could be done during first use of the app. The bot would introduce itself, and then list examples on screen of the types of questions it could answer.
- I decided against having a voice-only response pattern, as the cognitive load is very high for voice-only apps, and this is doubly true when dealing with numbers and trends.
- Any response from the bot had to be succint, as lengthy responses can make users feel like they aren't in control. Combining these with a GUI for the graphs, allowed users to further dig into the data.
- In order to not overwhelm users, I used progressive disclosure to show additional information, often by displaying a small prompt near a graph.

Challenges

- Current design tools like Sketch do not make it easy to design a Voice User Interface. It was hard to show the bot's speech alongside the interface.
- It is difficult to ascribe personality to a bot, especially during very pragmatic interactions.
- Error handling for VUI can cause users to be frustrated as the app often cannot make suggestions as it is able to with misspelled words. Users often may not know how to recover from an error, and this can lead them to abandon the app.
