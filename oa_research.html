Generative Research

Objective

To find out the attitudes and motivations of our Open Access users, and whether they differ from our subscription users.

Approach

- Examined the Google Analytics data in Data Studio to try and create meaningful segments for our Open Access users, around the frequency of visits and whether users from poorer institutes consumed and submitted more OA content.
- Worked closely with the UX Domain lead to script, run and analyse 8 moderated, interviews with participants from different parts of the world.
- Presented findings & insights to my team to further their understanding of the attitudes & motivations of Open Access users.
- Organised prioritising sessions with product managers & front-end developers to determine what experiments we would run first.
- Created a pattern library that would best display information for the different elements of the newsletter, such as callouts for user responses, various polls, special issue boxes on particular topics, and a “at a glance” view on breakthroughs.

Results

- Disseminated the findings to teams outside my own, including marketing intelligence and the wider business at a meet-and-greet session.
- Took the findings from the research into a team “reset day”, which helped steer us in the right direction, and used insights to back up OKR questions from management.
- Saw an improvement in the way the team communicated about features, by questioning whether we were building the right thing, showing that the research created more empathy and understanding of user behaviour.
